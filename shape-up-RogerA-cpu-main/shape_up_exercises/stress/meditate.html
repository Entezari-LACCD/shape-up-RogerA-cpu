What is Meditation?

Meditation is one path toward transforming the mind. Buddhist meditation
practices encourage and develop concentration, clarity, and emotional positivity.
When you begin a meditation practice, you may learn the patterns and habits of
your mind. Meditation offers you a way to cultivate new and more positive ways
to approach the things you do throughout your day. Meditation can have a
transformative effect and can lead to positive changes as well as a new
understanding of life.

Why Meditate?

  • Be happy: Meditation causes the pituitary gland in your brain to secrete endorphins that help elevate mood and have a positive effect on the whole body.
  • Reduce stress: Endorphins also reduce stress levels in the brain.
  • Focus: Through meditation you can learn self-discipline so your mind can focus with increased efficiency.
  • Sleep better: Scientific studies have shown that those who meditate enhance their slow wave sleep pattern which can help fight insomnia.
  • Lower blood pressure:Lower blood pressure: Research has also shown that meditation can reduce blood pressure, both in the short term and later in life.
  • Pain Relief: The long term practise of meditation can lead to physical changes in the brain that help alter the perception of pain.
